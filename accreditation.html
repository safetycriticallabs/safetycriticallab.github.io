Safety Critical Labs / Accreditation

The path to ISO/IEC
17065 accreditation.

Accreditation is the step that turns a credible third-party determination into a regulatory instrument. Safety Critical Labs has completed ANAB intake and scheme review, with formal engagement deferred until certification volume supports it.

[ What Accreditation Means ]

A recognized authority,
not just a credible voice.

ISO/IEC 17065 is the international standard for bodies that certify products, processes, and services. Accreditation under it is granted by a national accreditation body that has itself been audited against ISO/IEC 17011. In the United States, the recognized body is the ANSI National Accreditation Board (ANAB).

For Safety Critical Labs, accreditation does one specific thing. It moves the SCL mark from "a defensible third-party assessment against a published standard" to "an assessment whose conformity has been independently verified by a body that regulators already recognize."

The framework is open. The methodology is documented. The mark is filed for trademark protection. Accreditation is the missing piece that closes the loop between SCL determinations and the regulatory bodies whose recognition the mark is built to earn.

This is a multi-year path. It begins with documented quality management, demonstrated independence and impartiality, competency records for every assessor, and external audit. SCL has been preparing for this from the first version of the framework.

Current Status · July 2026

Intake and scheme review are complete. ANAB confirmed the submitted certification scheme is sufficient to proceed and provided a fee estimate for ISO/IEC 17065 accreditation of Safety Critical Labs. Formal engagement is deferred as a deliberate sequencing decision. Accreditation spend follows certification volume, and SCL will re-engage ANAB when that volume supports it.

[ The Path ]

From intake to accredited body.

Each step has a defined input and a defined output. The order is fixed. The timeline scales with the certification body's readiness, not the accreditor's.

01

Intake and scheme review

Submit the certification scheme to ANAB for initial review. Confirm the scope, the published standard, and the methodology are sufficient to proceed.

Complete
02

Fee estimate and engagement

ANAB prepares a formal fee estimate based on the scheme. When SCL accepts the engagement, formal accreditation activities begin.

Estimate received · Deferred
03

Gap assessment and document review

ANAB reviews SCL's quality management system, methodology, assessor qualifications, and impartiality controls against the requirements of ISO/IEC 17065.

Pending
04

On-site assessment

ANAB conducts a formal on-site assessment, including witness audit of at least one SCL certification engagement. Findings are documented and any nonconformities are remediated.

Pending
05

Accreditation decision

ANAB issues a formal accreditation determination. Accredited bodies are listed in the ANAB public directory and may use the accreditation mark in conjunction with the SCL certification mark.

Pending
06

Surveillance and reassessment

Accreditation is maintained through periodic surveillance audits and full reassessment on a defined cycle. The framework's continuous validation discipline applies to the certification body itself.

Pending
[ What the Mark Becomes ]

A certificate regulators
can reference.

Today, an SCL certificate is a defensible record. After accreditation, it becomes an instrument regulators can formally rely on.

Procurement officers can cite it. Aviation authorities can reference it in airworthiness criteria. Medical device reviewers can accept it as evidence. The accreditation does not change what the certificate means. It changes what regulators can do with it.

Every assessment SCL conducts before accreditation is conducted to the same standard that will be audited after accreditation. Accreditation does not raise the bar. It validates that the bar is already where it should be.
